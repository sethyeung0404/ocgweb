 Getting Jobs Abroad : IT Talents Wanted Globally

In Hong Kong, employees in the IT sector claim to be the bottom part of the food chain. But is that true? Indeed, employees in the IT sector have a much higher increment in salary than the average employees. The demand for IT talents is very high, locally or globally. They are considered skilled workers and have advantages in migration in many countries.

Taiwan

There is Employment Gold Card for employment-seeking individuals whose qualifications are reviewed and designated by the central competent authorities as Foreign Special Professionals. Professionals in the IT sector are included in the 7 categories of Foreign Special Professionals. The Employment Gold Card is a combination of a resident visa issued by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, a work permit issued by the Ministry of Labor and an Alien Resident Certificate ( ARC ) and a re-entry permit both issued by the NIA.



Germany

In European Countries, there are EU Blue Card for professional to work in the countries.

For Germany, people

  1. with a German or an accredited foreign or a university degree that is comparable to a German one AND
  2. have a working contract in so-called shortage occupations ( IT-skilled worker Included ) with the amount of €39.624 ( 3302 Euros per month ).
can apply for the German EU Blue Card which would be valid for 4 years or for the time of the working contract plus 3 months. Blue Card owner could apply for permanent residence after 33 months.



Workers in the IT sector are indeed largely demanded by the global market. If you are a Hong Kong Programmer looking for new opportunities, maybe going abroad would be a turning point of your career and life. Give it a shot!
